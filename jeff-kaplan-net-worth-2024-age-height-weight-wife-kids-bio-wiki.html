Career

Kaplan’s career kick-started when he Joined MMO that is Massively Multiplayer Online game Everquest. The game was accessed through an online platform known as Tagore. Following a successful run in MMO, he joined Legacy of Steel Guild.  His success and skills in gaming were spotted by Rob Pardo, another gaming guru. Rob Pardo was the Chief Creative Officer at Blizzard Entertainment and the brains behind the World of Warcraft. Kaplan thereafter got an opportunity of meeting Rob and employees of Blizzard. They also got to interact on the MMO of World of Warcraft that was still a working progress.

In 2002, Kaplan was hired to work as a guest designer on World of Warcraft after filing for an application. Additionally, after an outstanding work performance, Kaplan was promoted within a very short period. He became the new gaming director of World of Warcraft (Wow).  Later, he was transferred to work on a new project, an online game to be precisely known as Titan. However, the game was canceled.

Kaplan was soon afterward announced as the director of the newest Blizzard’s game known as Overwatch. Kaplan was a high achiever in his work and worked with so much excellence as the director of Overwatch. His track record proved to be a great asset to the Blizzard Company as he brought in millions of Overwatch players worldwide. Moreover, Blizzard’s revenue started growing by $1 billion within Overwatch’s first year.

Awards & Achievements

One of Kaplan’s greatest achievements includes making Overwatch one of the recognized games globally.  In addition to that, Kaplan was awarded the Vanguard Award in 2017 at The Fun, and Serious Game Festival held in Bilbao.
