FAQs about Sophie Raworth

Who is Sophie Raworth?

She is an English journalist, newsreader, and broadcaster known for her work with the BBC. She is a senior newsreader and one of the main presenters of BBC News at Six and BBC News at Ten.

What is Raworth’s educational background?

She was educated at Bute House Preparatory School, Putney High School, and St Paul’s Girls’ School. She holds a degree in French and German from the University of Manchester and a postgraduate diploma in broadcast journalism from City, University of London.

When did Raworth start her career with the BBC?

She joined the BBC in 1992 as a news reporter. She has since held various positions, including presenting BBC’s Breakfast News, BBC News at One, and other major news programs.

What notable programs has Sophie Raworth presented?

She has presented several notable programs, including BBC News at Six, BBC News at Ten, Watchdog, Crimewatch, and The Andrew Marr Show. She has also covered significant state occasions and election nights.
